# Interview Transcript — James, 44, Engineering Lead

**Date:** May 2026  
**Product:** B2B project management tool  
**Duration:** 42 minutes

---

Interviewer: How long have you been using the tool?

James: About two years now. We adopted it when the team grew past ten people. Before that we were just doing everything in Slack and GitHub issues.

Interviewer: What works well for you?

James: The integrations are solid. GitHub sync is reliable. I can see PRs linked to tasks which is genuinely useful. That part I have no complaints about.

Interviewer: What about what doesn't work?

James: The reporting. Every time someone asks me for a status update I have to go and manually pull together a summary. There's a reports section but it doesn't show what I need. It shows completion percentages which means nothing to me. I need to know what's blocked, what's at risk, and what's slipped from last week. None of that is surfaced.

Interviewer: What do you do instead?

James: I have a Notion doc I update every Friday. Just a running log. I go through the tool, look at everything that moved or didn't move that week, and write it up myself. My manager wants it by end of day Friday so I've built it into my routine. Probably takes 45 minutes.

Interviewer: Every week?

James: Every week. For two years. I keep thinking I'll find a way to automate it but I haven't gotten around to it. It's just faster to do it manually at this point.

Interviewer: Have you asked the vendor about this?

James: I submitted a feature request about 18 months ago. I got an email saying it was logged. Haven't heard anything since. I assume it's in a backlog somewhere. I've stopped expecting it to change.

Interviewer: If that Notion doc disappeared tomorrow, what would you do?

James: I'd probably just rebuild it. It's become how I think about the week. I don't think I could go back to just using the tool's view.

Interviewer: That's interesting — the doc has become the primary interface?

James: Yeah I guess it has. The tool is where the data lives. The doc is where I actually make sense of it. They're two different things serving two different purposes.
