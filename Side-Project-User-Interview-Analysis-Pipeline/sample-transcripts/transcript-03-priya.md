# Interview Transcript — Priya, 28, Product Designer

**Date:** May 2026  
**Product:** B2B project management tool  
**Duration:** 35 minutes

---

Interviewer: Walk me through a typical week with the tool.

Priya: I use it mainly for tracking design tasks and handoffs to engineering. It works for that. The kanban view is clean. I like being able to attach Figma links to tasks.

Interviewer: What does your Monday look like?

Priya: I check what engineering has moved to in-review over the weekend. Then I update my own tasks. Then I usually ping the PM on Slack to confirm priorities because sometimes things shift and the tool doesn't always reflect the latest thinking.

Interviewer: Why Slack rather than the tool?

Priya: Because that's where the conversation happens. The tool is for record-keeping. The actual decision of what we're doing this week happens on Slack or in standup. By the time it's in the tool it's already been decided.

Interviewer: Do you ever find the tool and Slack out of sync?

Priya: All the time. It's just kind of accepted. Like the tool is a lagging indicator of what's actually happening. Everyone knows that. We just update it when we get a chance.

Interviewer: What do you wish was different?

Priya: Honestly I wish the status updates were easier. Right now if something is blocked I have to write a comment, tag the person, change the status, maybe send a Slack message too just to make sure they see it. It's a lot of steps for something that should be a quick flag.

Interviewer: So you duplicate across both tools?

Priya: Yeah, basically. Anything important I put in both places. Because I can't be sure someone will check the tool. Slack I know they'll see.

Interviewer: If you had to give up one of them tomorrow, which would it be?

Priya: The project management tool, honestly. We'd survive on Slack and a shared doc. I know that's probably not what you want to hear.

Interviewer: No, that's really useful. Why would you keep Slack?

Priya: Because that's where the team actually is. The tool is where we're supposed to be. Those are different things.
